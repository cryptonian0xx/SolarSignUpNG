SolarSignupNG

Nigeria's Verified Solar Demand Registry

Registering genuine solar interest from homes and businesses to enable data-driven energy planning, reliable deployment, and future-ready sustainable energy systems for Nigeria.

Why We Started

SolarSignupNG was created during Nigeria's Solar Power Naija initiative under the National Electrification Project. The program aimed to expand solar access to 5 million households through private solar companies.

However, a critical gap emerged: while financing was available for solar companies (supply-side), there was no verified registry of Nigerian households and businesses genuinely interested in solar installations (demand-side).

Without visibility into real, qualified demand, deployment planning remained speculative, leaving many potential beneficiaries without access despite available funding.

Why Previous Programs Underperformed

Supply-Side Focus Without Demand Intelligence

Previous initiatives prioritized financing solar companies without mapping where real, qualified customers existed. This created a disconnect between available capacity and actual uptake.

Lack of Verified Beneficiary Database

Without a centralized registry of verified interest, solar companies lacked reliable pipelines, leading to high customer acquisition costs and inefficient deployment.

Most Nigerians Never Personally Experienced Solar

Without a mechanism to express genuine interest, the majority of potential beneficiaries remained invisible to implementers, perpetuating energy access gaps despite available solutions.

Our Repositioning: Solar Demand Registry

SolarSignupNG is now repositioned as Nigeria's neutral solar demand registry—a platform that aggregates, verifies, and qualifies solar interest to enable efficient, data-driven energy access expansion.

How It Works

  • Demand Aggregation: Register genuine interest from homes and businesses
  • Lead Qualification: Pre-assess feasibility based on property, usage, and location data
  • Market Intelligence: Provide solar companies with verified customer pipelines
  • Planning Support: Enable data-driven deployment for financiers, governments, and developers

Applications

  • Private solar company customer acquisition
  • Financing program design and targeting
  • ESG and carbon credit project development
  • Structured rollout planning for utilities and developers
  • Feasibility analysis for mini-grid and off-grid projects

Understanding Solar System Types

UPS-style Solar Systems: Typically power-bank-like systems providing short-duration backup (4-8 hours) for essential lights and appliances during grid outages.

Standalone Solar Systems: More comprehensive systems designed for longer autonomy (1-3 days), capable of powering multiple appliances with energy-aware usage patterns.
